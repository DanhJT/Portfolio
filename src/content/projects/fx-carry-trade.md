## Context

Empirical study of the carry trade in global FX markets. Currencies sorted each month into four equally weighted, non-overlapping portfolios based on their 1-month forward discounts, from the lowest (P1) to the highest (P4), to test whether forward discounts carry meaningful information for return generation.

## Approach

- Constructed **P1–P4 portfolios** by sorting currencies on 1-month forward discounts each month, with equal weighting and monthly rebalancing.
- Built a **long-short carry trade** going long P4 and short P1, capturing the spread between high- and low-forward-discount currencies.
- Also evaluated a **weighted carry strategy** as an alternative to equal-weighted long-short.
- Assessed performance through average excess return, volatility, Sharpe ratio, annualized Sharpe ratio, t-statistics, and p-values.

## Result

Strongest performance in P4, the P4 minus P1 long-short carry portfolio, and the weighted carry strategy, consistent with forward discounts carrying meaningful, statistically significant information for currency portfolio formation. Volatility and tail risk also rise with the carry, the well-known cost of the strategy.

**Key skills:** carry trade construction, currency portfolio sorting, forward discount analysis, risk-adjusted return evaluation, statistical testing, and Excel-based financial modeling.
