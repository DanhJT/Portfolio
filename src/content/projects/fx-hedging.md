## Context

A Swiss export business with CHF-denominated revenues and EUR-denominated operating costs is exposed to EUR/CHF movements through its margin. The brief: quantify that exposure, evaluate the impact of operational changes, and compare financial hedging structures against the unhedged baseline.

## Approach

- Built a scenario model estimating revenue, costs, taxes, profit margin, and operating cash flow under a range of **EUR/CHF exchange-rate scenarios**.
- Compared exposure **before and after operational changes**, including partial outsourcing of costs into CHF, to measure the effect of natural hedging. Matching part of the cost base with the revenue currency reduces, but does not eliminate, FX risk.
- Evaluated **financial hedging** through forward hedging and money-market hedging, calculating the firm's after-tax net CHF exposure and using it to estimate hedged operating cash flow under each structure.

## Result

A clear ranking of hedging alternatives against the unhedged baseline, with the residual exposure quantified after natural hedging and financial hedging are layered on. The model is structured so a treasurer can change a single FX or cost assumption and immediately see the effect on margin and cash flow.

**Key skills:** FX exposure analysis, scenario modeling, profit margin analysis, operating cash flow forecasting, natural hedging, forward hedging, money-market hedging, and Excel-based financial modeling.
